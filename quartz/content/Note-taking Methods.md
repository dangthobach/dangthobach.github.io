# Note-taking Methods & Vault Ideas

Based on research into Personal Knowledge Management (PKM), here are several popular note-taking methods and ideas for how to apply them to this Obsidian vault.

## 1. Popular Note-taking Methods

### Zettelkasten (Slip-box)
*   **Concept**: Create "atomic" notes (one idea per note) and link them together to form a web of knowledge.
*   **Best for**: Research, creative writing, and long-term learning.
*   **Workflow**: Fleeting Notes (quick captures) → Literature Notes (summaries in your own words) → Permanent Notes (final atomic ideas).

### PARA Method
*   **Concept**: Organize by *actionability* rather than topic.
*   **Structure**: 
    1.  **Projects**: Active tasks with a deadline.
    2.  **Areas**: Ongoing responsibilities (e.g., Health, Finances).
    3.  **Resources**: Topics of interest or reference material.
    4.  **Archives**: Completed or inactive items.
*   **Best for**: Project management and productivity.

### Linking Your Thinking (LYT) & Maps of Content (MOC)
*   **Concept**: Use "Maps of Content" (MOCs) as central hubs that link to related notes, rather than relying on deep folder structures.
*   **Best for**: Managing large vaults without feeling overwhelmed by folders.

### Cornell Method
*   **Concept**: A structured layout for active recall.
*   **Structure**: A main note-taking column, a "cue" column for questions/keywords, and a summary at the bottom.
*   **Best for**: Lectures, meetings, and textbook study.

## 2. Ideas for This Vault

Since this vault is currently in its early stages, here are some ways to start organizing:

### 📥 Create an "Inbox"
Start a dedicated folder or note called `Inbox` for "Fleeting Notes." This is where you can dump quick thoughts, links, or ideas before you decide where they belong.

### 🗺️ Build a "Home" Map of Content (MOC)
Instead of just a [[Welcome]] note, create a `Home.md` that acts as your dashboard. It can link to major categories like "Projects," "Learning," and "Personal."

### 📂 Apply a Simplified PARA Structure
You can create four top-level folders:
*   `10 Projects`
*   `20 Areas`
*   `30 Resources`
*   `40 Archives`

### 🏷️ Use Properties for Metadata
Use Obsidian Properties (YAML frontmatter) to track the status of your notes (e.g., `status: seedling`, `status: evergreen`) or to tag them by method (e.g., `method: zettelkasten`).

## 3. Recommended Getting Started Path
1.  **Capture**: Start by just writing. Don't worry about the "perfect" system yet.
2.  **Link**: When you mention a concept that could be its own note, use `[[WikiLinks]]`.
3.  **Organize Later**: Once you have 20–30 notes, you'll naturally see patterns. That's the best time to choose between PARA, Zettelkasten, or a hybrid.
